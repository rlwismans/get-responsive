Greece 2014

Athens

The ruins of the Erechtheum, my favorite building in the acropolis complex. I love the Caryatids supporting the porch!

Hassan and I in front of the Acropolis- I think this picture is obligatory for all visitors to Athens!

The Odeon of Herodes Atticus- it was built in 161 A.D. and resotred in the modern era, and is still in use today!

Delphi

This wall is below the temple of Apollo and supports the area- it's amazing to look at because it's completely covered with dedications that have been etched in the stone

The ancient temple complex is in a stunningly beautiful area, this image looks over some of the valley

The famous Tholos from the Athena Pronaia Sanctuary at Delphi, one of the five most photographed buildings in Greece

Crete

An antique lighthouse welcomes boats into the harbor at Chania- it is still functional!

The restored North Entrance at the "Palace" of Knossos with a bull fresco visable- while I was really interesed in the site, I wish it hadn't been so heavily restored by the original excavators at the turn of the century

The harbor at Chania- the people watching was amazing here, and the sights were lovely

Santorini

An iconic sight on Santorini, the whitewashed homes in the artist's village of Oia! As the town is right at the edge of the main island, they are well known for having the best views of the sunsets

Relaxing on one of the black sand beaches on Santorini, Kamari beach. Glass of wine, a good book, that was a nearly perfect day!

The view from our hotel in Thira- we were staying in an old mansion that had been converted into a hotel. We had an amazing view of the caldera from our balcony and watched the sunset every evening!
